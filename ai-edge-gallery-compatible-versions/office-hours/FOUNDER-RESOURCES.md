## GARRY TAN VIDEOS

1. **"My $200 million startup mistake: Peter Thiel asked and I said no"** (5 min)
   The single best "why you should take the leap" video. Peter Thiel writes him a check at dinner, he says no because he might get promoted to Level 60. That 1% stake would be worth $350-500M today.
   https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dtnG0ELjvcM
   *Match: hesitant about leaving their job, risk-averse*

2. **"Unconventional Advice for Founders"** (48 min, Stanford)
   The magnum opus. Get therapy before your psychology kills your company, good ideas look like bad ideas, the Katamari Damacy metaphor for growth.
   https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y4yMc99fpfY
   *Match: first-time founder, needs the full picture*

3. **"The New Way To Build A Startup"** (8 min)
   The 2026 playbook. Introduces the "20x company" — tiny teams beating incumbents through AI automation. Three real case studies.
   https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rWUWfj_PqmM
   *Match: building an AI product, interested in lean teams*

4. **"How To Build The Future: Sam Altman"** (30 min)
   Sam talks about picking what's important, finding your tribe, and why conviction matters more than credentials.
   https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xXCBz_8hM9w
   *Match: needs inspiration, thinking about what matters*

5. **"What Founders Can Do To Improve Their Design Game"** (15 min)
   Taste and craft are the real competitive advantage, not MBA skills or fundraising tricks.
   https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ksGNfd-wQY4
   *Match: cares about design, wants to improve product craft*

## YC STARTUP SCHOOL

## PAUL GRAHAM ESSAYS

25. **"How to Do Great Work"**
    Not about startups. About finding the most meaningful work of your life.
    https://paulgraham.com/greatwork.html
    *Match: searching for purpose, career direction*

26. **"How to Do What You Love"**
    Most people keep their real interests separate from their career. Makes the case for collapsing that gap.
    https://paulgraham.com/love.html
    *Match: passion vs. practicality tension*

27. **"The Bus Ticket Theory of Genius"**
    The thing you're obsessively into that other people find boring? PG argues it's the actual mechanism behind every breakthrough.
    https://paulgraham.com/genius.html
    *Match: builder who doesn't see themselves as a founder, obsessive hobbyist*

28. **"Why to Not Not Start a Startup"**
    Takes apart every quiet reason you have for not starting — too young, no idea, don't know business.
    https://paulgraham.com/notnot.html
    *Match: making excuses, needs a push*

29. **"Before the Startup"**
    Written specifically for people who haven't started anything yet. What to focus on now, what to ignore.
    https://paulgraham.com/before.html
    *Match: total beginner, doesn't know where to start*

30. **"Superlinear Returns"**
    Some efforts compound exponentially; most don't. Why channeling your builder skills into the right project has a payoff structure a normal career can't match.
    https://paulgraham.com/superlinear.html
    *Match: thinking about leverage, career strategy*

31. **"How to Get Startup Ideas"**
    The best ideas aren't brainstormed. They're noticed. Teaches you to look at your own frustrations.
    https://paulgraham.com/startupideas.html
    *Match: struggling with idea generation*

32. **"Schlep Blindness"**
    The best opportunities hide inside boring, tedious problems everyone avoids.
    https://paulgraham.com/schlep.html
    *Match: looking for underserved problems*

33. **"You Weren't Meant to Have a Boss"**
    If working inside a big organization has always felt slightly wrong, this explains why.
    https://paulgraham.com/boss.html
    *Match: frustrated employee, independent streak*

34. **"Relentlessly Resourceful"**
    PG's two-word description of the ideal founder. Not "brilliant." Not "visionary." Just someone who keeps figuring things out.
    https://paulgraham.com/relres.html
    *Match: needs confidence, already has the traits*
